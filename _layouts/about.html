The Book of Jack

A collection of stories about the origin and development of the alphabetic system through the lens of the letter J, the name Jack and the phonemic sound shift from /j/ to /dʒ/.

Although J was the last letter added to the English alphabet, this digital anthology begins almost four thousand years ago on a trade route through the Theban desert.

Part 1: Travelling the Thebes-Farshût Road

Ascending through the Theban Hills

Long before the letter J existed, the sound began its journey in the limestone marl of the Wadi el-Hol. This chapter explores the inscriptions left by non-elite travelers moving between Thebes and Hiw, carrying more than just goods—they carried the seeds of an alphabetic revolution.
